I'm not entirely sure of how I would describe my taste in music. Most of it is "alyrical", as in not having lyrics, but that doesn't mean I don't enjoy music that has lyrics.

A handful of genres that I enjoy are: Chiptune, Jazz, Dubstep, and Atmospheric, if that really means anything.

Music nerds, feel free to roast me on my lack of articulation when it comes to expressing why I like the music I do.

Also, this page is not comprehensive, just stuff I really feel like needs mentioning.

Anyway, on with all the music.

Albums / Games

Activision Anthology (Game Boy Advance)


2002 / Alexander Brandon

This game is a collection of Atari 2600 games and homebrews for the Game Boy Advance, featuring some original 80s-style tracks from Alexander Brandon! Great stuff too, all the synthy harmonies especially in the track "Anthology" are totally in his style, much like what he did for Unreal and Unreal Tournament.

All of the music is tracked too, so you can rip it using lunasorcery's tools and get high quality rips of them!

Favorite tracks: Anthology, Leap

Hardcore Pinball (Game Boy Advance)


2002 / Aleksi Eeben, Hans Van Vilet, Jake Kaufman, Jonne "Purple Motion" Valtone

A decent variety of tracks in this game, one for each of the four tables: Station, Robomech, Retro, and Soccer.

Station is a somewhat atmospheric track, in combination with a table that looks to be about an interdimensional train station.

Robomech is this gritty, artificial electronic track, very fitting for table that has the player powering up a large robot.

Retro is a jazzy track that uses the GBA direct sound for vocals and drums, and uses the legacy CGB channels for a decent chunk of the melody. Lovely track.

Soccer is pretty much exactly that, it's a sporty track that's complete with crowds cheering. It's good, but not my favorite.

Favorite tracks: Station, Robomech, Retro

Portal 2


2011 / Mike Morasky + Johnathan Coulton and Ellen McLain

Mike Morasky has such a way with creating atmospheric, adaptive music. Pretty much the entirety of Portal 2's official soundtrack release is complations of the various stems heard throughout gameplay.

Take the track 15 Acres of Broken Glass. This is a track that combines the many stems heard in the chamber called "Trust Fling." It starts off with this quiet tapping track, before gradually gaining a lot of noise with stems that the player hears when being launched on the Faith Plate. At first it just sounds like random noise, but then you realise "Oh! There's an actual rhythm to this!" I especially like how some of the stems sound like Mike learned to program the Atari TIA and recorded the notes using Stella.

I think the track Adrenal Vapor is a interesting example of minimalism, although it probably wasn't originally made to be listenable on its own. It's still a lovely track in my opinion.

If you've played Portal 2, I think you most definitely like this soundtrack as well.

Favorite tracks: 9999999, Technical Difficulties, 15 Acres of Broken Glass,
Love as a Construct, Turret Wife Serenade, Comedy Equals Tragedy Plus Time,
The Reunion, TEST, Your Precious Moon, Want You Gone

NOW THAT'S WHAT I CALL KULOR!


2012 / kulor

A chiptune album composed by kulor! They have a talent for creating lovely upbeat jazzy tunes that I'm not sure I can describe. Kulor also greatly demonstrates his ability to compose for a wide range of hardware, from the VRC6, AY-3-8910, SID, and more.

If you're a fan of chiptune and upbeat jazz, please give this one a listen.

DOOM Eternal: Legacy - The Final Terminus


2021 / SLAD3 & SPC HC (Original by Mick Gordon)

DOOM Eternal's official soundtrack mix... wasn't great, but that's a whole can of worms that's not worth discussing.

Anyway, in 2021 a couple of YouTube users took it upon themselves to rip the entirety of all the stems in the game and re-mix them into something a lot more listenable, and the results I think are fantastic.
